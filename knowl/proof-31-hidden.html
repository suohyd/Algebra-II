Consider the squaring map which is a group homomorphism:
\begin{equation*} \varphi\colon\mathbb{F}_p^\times\to\mathbb{F}_p^\times\quad\text{given by}\quad x\mapsto x^2. \end{equation*}
The kernel is \(\{\pm 1\}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(|{\rm Im}(\varphi)|=\tfrac{p-1}{2}\text{,}\) i.e., \([\mathbb{F}_p^\times:{\rm Im}(\varphi)]=2\text{.}\) This implies that \(a\,{\rm Im}(\varphi)\cdot b\,{\rm Im}(\varphi)=ab\,{\rm Im}(\varphi)={\rm Im}(\varphi)\text{,}\) i.e., \(ab\in{\rm Im}(\varphi)\text{,}\) i.e., \(ab\in\mathbb{F}_p^{\times 2}\text{.}\)
