Item 1. [Well-definedness].

Suppose that \(a/b=c/d\text{,}\) i.e., there exists \(e\in A\setminus\{0\}\) such that \(e(ad-bc)=0\in A\) (refer LemmaÂ 6.2.1). Since \(A\) is an integral domain, \(ad=bc\text{.}\) Thus, \(f(a)f(d)=f(b)f(c)\text{.}\) By the assumption \(f\) is an monomorphism, \(b\neq 0\) and \(c\neq 0\text{.}\) Hence, \(f(a)f(b)^{-1}=f(c)f(d)^{-1}\text{.}\)
