Example 2.2.6. (Homomorphisms from simple rings).

Let \(R\) be a simple ring (see DefinitionÂ 1.5.10) and \(S\) be a nonzero ring with unity. Any ring homomorphism \(\varphi\colon R\to S\) is a ring monomorphism. Indeed, for any ring homomorphism \(\ker(\varphi)\) is a two-sided ideal of \(R\text{.}\) Since \(R\) is a simple ring and \(\varphi\) is nonzero, the kernel of \(\varphi\text{,}\) \(\ker(\varphi)=\{0\}\text{,}\) i.e., \(\varphi\) is a monomorphism.
In particular a ring homomorphism from a division ring or a field to a nonzero ring with unity is a ring monomorphism.
