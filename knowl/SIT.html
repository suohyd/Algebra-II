Theorem 3.2.4. (Second Isomorphism Theorem).

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(S\) be a subring of \(R\text{,}\) and let \(I\) be an ideal in \(R\text{.}\) The set
\begin{equation*} S+I=\{s+x:s\in S\text{ and }x\in I\} \end{equation*}
is a subring of \(R\text{,}\) and the mapping
\begin{equation*} \varphi\colon (S+I)/I\to S/(S\cap I)\quad\text{defined by}\quad s+I\mapsto s+(S\cap I),\;\text{where }s\in S; \end{equation*}
is a ring isomorphism.
