Proof

In view of Lemma 4.9.1 we assume that \(-1\) is not a square in \(\mathbb{F}_p\text{.}\) Note that if \(-1\) is not a square then either \(2\) or \(- 2\) are squares in \(\mathbb{F}_p\text{.}\) Indeed if \(-1\) and \(2\) (resp., \(-2\)) is not a square then, by Lemma 4.9.2, their product \(-2\) (resp., \(2\)) is a square. If \(\pm 2\in\mathbb{F}_p^{\times2}\text{,}\) then we have, by (4.9.1), \(x^4+1\) is reducible over \(\mathbb{F}_p^\times\text{.}\)
