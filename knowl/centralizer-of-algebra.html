Example 1.9.8. (Centralizer).

Let \(A\) be an \(F\)-algebra and \(S\) be a nonempty subset of \(A\text{.}\) The centralizer of \(S\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} C_A(S)=\{a\in A:as=sa\quad\text{for all}\;s\in S\} \end{equation*}
is an \(F\)-algebra. This is a subring of \(A\) (refer to ExampleÂ 1.6.1). Verify that \(C_A(S)\) is a vector subspace of \(A\text{.}\) Since \(A\) is an \(F\)-algebra the identity (1.4.1) is satisfied by \(C_A(S)\text{.}\) Hence, centralizer is an \(F\)-subalgebra.
