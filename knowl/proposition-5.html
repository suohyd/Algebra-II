Proposition 3.3.5.

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and let \(\mfa\) be an ideal in \(A\text{.}\) Then there is a one-one correspondence between prime ideals of \(A\) containing \(\mfa\) and prime ideals of \(A/\mfa\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \left\{\mfp:\mfp\text{ is a prime ideal containing }\mfa\right\}\longleftrightarrow{}\left\{\text{prime ideals in }A/\mfa\right\}\quad\text{It is defined by}\quad\mfp\mapsto\mfp/\mfa. \end{equation*}
Similarly, there is a one-one correspondence between maximal ideals of \(A\) containing \(\mfa\) and maximal ideals of \(A/\mfa\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \left\{\mfm:\mfm\text{ is a maximal ideal containing }\mfa\right\}\longleftrightarrow{}\left\{\text{maximal ideals in }A/\mfa\right\}\quad\text{It is defined by}\quad\mfm\mapsto\mfm/\mfa. \end{equation*}

Proof.
