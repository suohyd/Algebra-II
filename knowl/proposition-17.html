Proposition 6.2.3.

We keep the notations of LemmaÂ 6.2.1. The set
\begin{equation*} S^{-1}A=\left\{a/s:a\in A\,\text{and}\,s\in S\right\} \end{equation*}
with addition and multiplication defined below is a commutative ring.
  1. \((a/s)+(b/t)=(at+bs)/st\) for \(a,b\in A\) and \(s,t\in S\)
  2. \((a/s)\cdot (b/s)=ab/st\) for \(a,b\in A\) and \(s,t\in S\text{.}\)
  3. The additive identity of \(S^{-1}A\) is \(0_A/1_A\) while the multiplicative identity of \(S^{-1}A\) is \(1_A/1_A\text{.}\)
