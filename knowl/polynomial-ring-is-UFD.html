Theorem 4.5.8. (Unique factorization in polynomial ring in one variable over a field).

Let \(F\) be a field and let \(F[X]\) be the polynomial ring over \(F\) in one variable. Then every non-constant polynomial \(f\in F[X]\) can be written as a product of finite number of irreducible polynomials \(p_i\in F[X]\text{,}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} f=p_1p_2\cdots p_n\quad\text{where}\;p_i\;\text{are not necessarily distinct}. \end{equation*}
Furthermore, the above expression is unique up to associates, i.e., if \(f=q_1q_2\cdots q_m\) an another factorization of \(f\) into irreducibles then \(m=n\) and there is some renumbering of \(p_i\) so that \(p_i\) associate of \(q_i\text{.}\)
