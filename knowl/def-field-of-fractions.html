Example 6.2.6. (Field of fractions/Quotient field).

Let \(A\) be an integral domain. The set \(S=A\setminus\{0\}\) is a multiplicatively closed subset of \(A\text{.}\) The ring \(S^{-1}A\) is a field and it is called the field of fractions of \(A\) or the quotient field of \(A\). Note that it is a particular case of ExampleÂ 6.2.5 with the prime ideal \((0)\text{.}\)
The field of rational numbers \(\Q\) is obtained as the field of fractions of \(\Z\text{.}\)
