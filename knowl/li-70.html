Item 3. [Injectivity].

Suppose that \(f(x+n\Z)=0\text{,}\) i.e., \(x\in p_i^{a_i}\Z\) for each \(i\text{.}\) In other words, \(x\in\bigcap_{i=1}^{r}p_i^{a_i}\Z\text{.}\) Since \(lcm(p_1^{a_1},\ldots,p_r^{a_r})=n\text{,}\) by LemmaÂ 4.1.1, we get that \(x\in n\Z\text{.}\) Hence \(f\) is injective.
