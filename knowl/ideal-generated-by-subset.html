Definition 1.7.1. (Ideal generated by a subset).

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(S\) be a subset of \(R\text{.}\) The left ideal generated by \(S\) (resp., right ideal generated by \(S\) , ideal generated by \(S\)) is denoted by \((S)\) and it is defined as follows.
\begin{align*} \text{Left ideal generated by }S:\quad\amp \left\{\sum_{i=1}^n x_is_i:x_i\in R, s_i\in S\text{ and }n\in\N\right\};\\ \text{Right ideal generated by }S:\quad\amp \left\{\sum_{i=1}^n s_iy_i:y_i\in R, s_i\in S\text{ and }n\in\N\right\};\\ \text{Ideal generated by }S:\quad\amp \left\{\sum_{i=1}^n x_is_iy_i:x_i,y_i\in R, s_i\in S\text{ and }n\in\N\right\}. \end{align*}
