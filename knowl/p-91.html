First observe that an irreducible element in \(A\) remains irreducible in \(A[x]\text{.}\) Thus in order to show \(A[x]\) is a UFD we first show that any nonzero nonunit polynomial \(f\in A[x]\) can be factored into a product of irreducibles. So we assume that \(f\in A[x]\) is a nonzero nonunit. By LemmaÂ 7.1.4, there exists a primitive polynomial \(f_1\in A[x]\) such that \(f=C(f)f_1\text{.}\) Since \(A\) is UFD, \(C(f)\) can be uniquely factored into irreducibles in \(A\text{.}\) Thus we further assume that \(f\in A[x]\) is a primitive reducible polynomial. So there are \(g,h\in A[x]\) such that \(f=gh\text{,}\) and \(\deg g\lt\deg f\) and \(\deg h\lt\deg f\text{.}\) By induction on \(\deg f\) we get \(g,h\) can be factored in \(A[x]\text{,}\) thus \(f\) can be factored in a product of irreducibles in \(A[x]\text{.}\)
