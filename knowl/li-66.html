Item 2.

Suppose that \(n\) is a prime number. For any \(1\leq i\lt n\text{,}\) \(\gcd(i,n)=1\) and hence \(\Z=i\Z+n\Z\) (see LemmaÂ 4.1.1(3)), i.e., there exists \(x,y\in\Z\) with \(1=ix+ny\text{.}\) Therefore, \([1]=[i][x]\in\Z/n\Z\text{,}\) i.e., \([i]\) is invertible. Converse follows from the part (1) of this proposition.
