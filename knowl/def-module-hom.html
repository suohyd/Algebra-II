Definition 9.5.1.

Let \(R\) be a ring. Let \(M\) and \(N\) are left \(R\)-modules. A map \(f\colon M\to N\) is said to be an \(R\)-module homomorphism if for any \(m,m^\prime\in M\) and any \(r\in R\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(m+m^\prime)=f(m)+f(m^\prime)\quad\text{and}\quad f(r\cdot m)=r\cdot f(m). \end{equation*}
