Let \(R\) be a ring and \(R^{\rm op}\) be its opposite ring (see ExampleÂ 1.2.9). If \(M\) is a left \(R\)-module (resp., a right \(R\)-module) then \(M\) is a right \(R^{\rm op}\)-module (resp., a left \(R^{\rm op}\)-module). Indeed, given a left \(R\)-module \(M\) we define \(M\times R^{\rm op}\to M\) by
\begin{equation*} (m,r^{\rm op})\mapsto r\cdot m. \end{equation*}
