Now we show the converse. Suppose that \(a\in F\) is an eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\) Thus there exists \(0\neq v\in V\) such that \(T(v)=av\text{.}\) By division algorithm there exists a constant \(c\in F\) and \(q(x)\in F[x]\) such that
\begin{equation*} m_T(x)=q(x)(x-a)+c. \end{equation*}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} 0=m_T(T)(v)=q(T)\left((T-a\unit_V)(v)\right)+c\cdot v=c\cdot v. \end{equation*}
Since \(v\neq 0\) we get that \(c=0\in F\) and hence \(x-a\mid m_T(x)\text{,}\) i.e., \(a\in F\) is a root of \(m_T(x)\text{.}\)
