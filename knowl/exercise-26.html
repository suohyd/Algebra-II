Exercise 1.

Let \(F\) be a field. Show that a polynomial \(f(X)\) in \(F[X]\) of degree \(2\) or \(3\) is reducible in \(F[X]\) if and only if \(f(X)\) has a root in \(F\text{.}\) Give an example of a polynomial of degree \(4\) over a field \(F\) which is reducible in \(F[X]\) but it has no root in \(F\text{.}\)
