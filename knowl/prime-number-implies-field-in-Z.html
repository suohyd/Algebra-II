Proposition 4.1.2.

Let \(n\Z\) be an ideal of \(\Z\text{,}\) where \(n\in\N\text{.}\) Then we have the following.
  1. If \(n\) is not a prime number then \(\Z/n\Z\) is not an integral domain.
  2. The ring \(\Z/n\Z\) is a field if and only if \(n\) is prime.
  3. The group of units of \(\Z/n\Z\text{,}\) \(\left(\Z/n\Z\right)^\times=\{[i]\in\Z/n\Z:\gcd(i,n)=1\}.\)
