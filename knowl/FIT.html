Theorem 3.2.3. (First Isomorphism Theorem).

Let \(\varphi\colon R\to S\) be an epimorphism of rings.
  1. If \(I\) is an ideal in \(R\) then \(\varphi(I)\) is an ideal in \(S\text{.}\)
  2. [First Isomorphism Theorem].
    If \(I\) is an ideal in \(R\) containing \(\ker(\varphi)\) then
    \begin{equation*} \overline{\varphi}\colon R/I\to S/\varphi(I)\quad\text{given by}\quad r+I\mapsto\varphi(r)+\varphi(I) \end{equation*}
    is an isomorphism of rings.
