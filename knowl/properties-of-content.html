Lemma 7.1.4.

Let \(f(x)=a_0+a_1x+\cdots+a_nx^n\in A[x]\text{.}\)
  1. For \(\alpha\in A\text{,}\) \(C(\alpha f)=\alpha C(f)\text{.}\)
  2. There exists a primitive polynomial \(f_1\in A[x]\) such that \(f=C(f)f_1\) and \(\deg f=\deg f_1\text{.}\)
  3. If \(f\) is a nonconstant irreducible polynomial then \(f\) is primitive. The converse is not true.
