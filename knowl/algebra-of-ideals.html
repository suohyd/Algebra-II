Lemma 1.7.2.

Let \(\mathfrak{a}\) and \(\mathfrak{b}\) be left ideals (resp., right ideals, two-sided ideals) in a ring \(R\text{.}\) Then following are left ideals (resp., right ideals, two-sided ideals):
  1. [Smallest left ideal containing both \(\mfa\) and \(\mfb\)].
    \(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b}=\{a+b:a\in\mathfrak{a}\text{ and }b\in\mathfrak{b}\}\text{.}\) More generally if \(\mfa_i\) are left ideals of \(R\) then
    \begin{equation*} \sum\mfa_i=\left\{\sum_ix_i:x_i\in\mfa_i\text{ and all but finitely many}\quad x_i=0\right\}. \end{equation*}
  2. [Smallest left ideal contained in both \(\mfa\) and \(\mfb\)].
    \(\mfa\cap\mfb\)
  3. [Left ideal generated by \(\mfa\mfb\)].
    \(\mfa\mfb=\left(\{ab:a\in\mfa,b\in\mfb\}\right)\)
  4. [Powers of a left ideal].
    \(\mfa^n=(\{a^n:a\in\mfa\text{ and }a^n=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdots a}_{n\text{-times}}\})\) when \(n\gt 0\) and we assume \(\mfa^0=(1)=R\text{.}\)
  5. If \(\mfa,\mfb\) and \(\mathfrak{c}\) are ideals (i.e., two-sided ideals) in \(R\) then we have following identities.
    \begin{align*} \mfa(\mfb+\mfc)\amp=\mfa\mfb+\mfa\mfc\\ (\mfa+\mfb)\mfc\amp=\mfa\mfc+\mfb\mfc\\ \text{If}\;\mfb\subseteq\mfa\;\text{or}\;\mfc\subseteq\mfa\quad\text{then}\quad\amp\mfa\cap(\mfb+\mfc)=(\mfa\cap\mfb)+(\mfa\cap\mfc) \end{align*}
