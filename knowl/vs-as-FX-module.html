Example 9.2.4. (Vector space as an \(F[X]\)-module).

Let \(V\) be a vector space over a field \(F\text{,}\) and fix an \(F\)-linear map \(T\in\End_F(V)\text{.}\) Recall that \(T^r\) is the linear map given by \(\underbrace{T\circ\cdots\circ T}_{r-\text{times}}\text{.}\) We define a map
\begin{equation*} F[X]\times V\to V \end{equation*}
by
\begin{equation*} (a_0+a_1X+\cdots+a_nX^n,v)\mapsto a_0v+a_1T(v)+a_2T^2(v)+\cdots+a_nT^n(v). \end{equation*}
It is left to the reader to verify that this makes \(V\) a left \(F[X]\)-module.
