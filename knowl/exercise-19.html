Exercise 5.

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, and let \(\mfa\) be an ideal in \(A\text{.}\) Define
\begin{equation*} {\rm rad}(\mfa)=\{a\in A:a^n\in\mfa\quad{\text{for some natural number}\;n}\;\text{that may depend on}\;a\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Show that \({\rm rad}(\mfa)\) is an ideal in \(A\text{.}\) Further show that if \(\mfp\) is a prime ideal in \(A\) then \({\rm rad}(\mfp)=\mfp\text{.}\)
