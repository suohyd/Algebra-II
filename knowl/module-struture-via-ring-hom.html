Example 9.2.7. (Induced module structure via a ring homomorphism).

Let \(\varphi\colon R\to S\) be a ring homomorphism, and let \(N\) be a left \(S\)-module. We show that \(N\) can be considered as a left \(R\)-module. Indeed, define
\begin{equation*} R\times N\to N\quad\text{by}\quad (r,n)\mapsto\varphi(r)\cdot n\text{.} \end{equation*}
Verify that this makes \(N\) a left \(R\)-module.
